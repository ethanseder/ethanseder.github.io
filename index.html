Projects

Bike Safety Airbag

I am developing an airbag for bicyclists. The system is designed to be integrated into the shoulder straps of the user’s backpack. The system is capable of senses impending impacts and deploys within 200 ms to protect the user. The airbag is designed to protect the neck, clavicle, ribs, and shoulder.

Check out a video of it in action

SEAS Racing Team

I have been a part of Harvard’s SEAS racing team for two years. We are building a fuel-efficient vehicle to compete in the SAE Super-mileage Competition. Last year, I designed and manufactured the rear wheel and transmission to minimize power loss. In June 2018, we placed the highest of any first-year teams.

As President of the team, I have handled administrative duties such as scheduling, budgeting, and complying with regulations. From machining to carbon fiber manufacturing, I am working hard to engineer our race car to be fast and fuel efficient.

ES51 Robot Competition

ES51: Computer Aided Machine Design focuses on the design and construction of mechanical and electromechanical devices. I learned engineering graphics, sketching, dimensioning, tolerancing, materials selection, structural design, and the necessity of precision in design and manufacturing. Our team designed and manufactured a robot that won 2nd place in the class competition.
