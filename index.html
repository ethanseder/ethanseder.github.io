SEAS Racing Team

One of the projects I have been a part of here at Harvard is the SEAS racing team. We are building a fuel-efficient vehicle to compete in the SAE Supermileage Competition. On the team this past year I designed and manufactured the rear wheel and transmission to minimize power loss. In 2018 we placed the best of best of any first year team. Cars have always been a passion of mine. This team has allowed me to put into action the many hours I spent learning principles in the classroom. From heavy machining to carbon fiber manufacturing I am building things I have always dreamed of.

ES51 Robot Competition

One of the classes I have taken here at Harvard that taught me about the precision needed in design and manufacturing was ES51: Computer Aided Machine Design. It is focused on the design and construction of mechanical and electromechanical devices. It teaches engineering graphics, sketching, dimensioning, tolerancing, materials selection, and structural design. In the class we were separated into teams and then given the task to manufacture a robot for a competition using only parts that we designed and manufactured. My team ended up getting 2nd place overall.
